Ensuring reliability into the future

Tri-State and our members are building an electric system for the future that maintains reliability and enhances resilience at competitive rates for our membership. As part of Tri-State’s ongoing system planning processes, we perform detailed modeling to identify the types and locations of new resources that will advance our mission to serve our members a reliable, affordable and responsible supply of electricity.

Voice your support for Tri-State’s plan to ensure reliable, responsible power

We all know how reliable power is critical to our rural way of life. Tri-State has filed a preferred portfolio with the Colorado Public Utilities Commission (Commission) that preserves reliability at the least cost, while increasing renewable energy supply and decreasing emissions.

Tri-State’s preferred portfolio, which we have asked the Commission to approve, is central to delivering a responsible electricity system for the future. Our preferred portfolio presents the least-cost mix of diverse resources, including new wind and solar power projects, our first battery storage systems, and a new natural gas power plant with hydrogen-blend capability, to reliably serve growing rural energy needs. Our planning demonstrates that we will always have the power our members need, even during extreme weather events, and meet all emissions reduction and renewable energy requirements.
