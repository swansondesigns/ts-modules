Reliability is more than a promise

At Tri-State G&T, reliability is more than a promise—it’s our mission. We understand that dependable power is the backbone of our the rural communities we serve. Our members know they can rely on us for the power they need today, tomorrow, and for generations to come. This remains an unwavering promise even as we navigate the energy transition.

Uniting with our members, we invest in modernizing grids, equipment, and systems for reliability our members can trust. Additionally, our renewable energy efforts reinforce our dependent resiliency. As a cooperative, we commit to protecting wholesale electric energy without sacrificing affordability.

Our members can count on us for the future, because whatever the future holds, we’ll power it.

Protecting a basic need

We are deeply committed to the safety and well-being of the communities we serve and are at the forefront of reducing the risk of wildfire by taking measures that ensure our utility infrastructure is not the origin or contributing factor for a wildfire.

Our wildfire mitigation plans exist to safeguard our communities and protect our transmission system, ensuring the delivery of safe, reliable and affordable electricity.

Planning for the future, together

We strive for a resilient grid now and for decades to come. By planning for our future with our Electric Resource Plan (ERP), our members can always rely on stable power. The ERP showcases how Tri-State ensures our transition will continue to meet the growing needs of our members.

The resource planning process serves our members’ needs in the most affordable way while meeting our regulatory requirements. We develop and assess alternative scenarios based on a number of factors. The plans are compared on the basis of cost, reliability, and environmental characteristics.

Transmission that connects the rural West

To get our energy to our members, we power our local electric utilities through electric power transmission. It works by carrying Tri-State’s wholesale electric energy to our members’ electrical substations through transmission lines. Secure transmission lines provide power to our members, avoiding the loss of high voltage electricity over long distances.

Our Energy Mix

Energy mix refers to the combination of different energy sources—like solar, wind, coal, natural gas, and more—that an electric cooperative uses to generate power. Combining a number of different energy sources leads to increased reliability with renewable energy sources.
