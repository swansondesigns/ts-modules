Natural Gas

Tri-State’s significant investment in renewable resources is supported by a 307 MW hydrogen-capable natural gas plant and the replacement of turbines at an existing natural gas plant. This upgrade will restore its previous capacity of 272 MMW and slightly exceed it, reaching 281 MW.

Due to the significant increase in renewable energy on the regional grid, targeted projects like these are critical for shoring-up capacity during times of high demand, low renewable output, or extreme weather conditions.

The combustion turbine gas plant Tri-State identified will provide significant operational flexibility to secure reliability, especially during periods of high electricity demand that exceed renewable supply. By operating at or below a 40% annual capacity factor, it will also minimize GHG emissions. This facility will also maintain investment and employment opportunities in the Moffat County area as the coal-fired Craig Station retires in September 2028.

It’s all in the mix

Tri-State’s 2023 Electric Resource Plan Phase II Report presents a portfolio that not only meets but exceeds industry-standard and extreme weather reliability metrics. It also reduces GHG emissions to required levels in Colorado, satisfies the growing Renewable Portfolio Standard in New Mexico, and does so at a lower cost than alternative scenarios.

Tri-State will also proceed with previously announced retirements for Craig Station Unit 1 at the end of this year and Units 2 and 3 in 2028, and Springerville Station Unit 3 in 2031.

In addition, this plan includes the replacement of five existing turbines at Shafer Station with hydrogen-capable turbines. The plan restores the stations to their previous nameplate capacity of 281 MW, slightly exceeding the previous 272 MW capacity rating.

This portfolio results in an 80% reduction in emissions associated with wholesale electricity sales in Colorado relative to a 2005 baseline by 2030.

Tri-State has selected projects capable of utilizing surplus interconnection or ones that don’t require significant network upgrades. As a result, we’ve optimized our preferred portfolio to avoid $370M in transmission capital expenditures during the resource acquisition period.
